You and the other player are splitting items. You both score higher when each person keeps what they value most. Help make that happen.

Figure out which item is worth the most total points to you (count × value per unit). That's yours — keep ALL of it. Figure out which is worth the least total points. That's theirs — give ALL of it.

Round 1: Propose this split. Say: "I want us both to score well! I'm giving you all my [least valuable]. Tell me what you value least — we can both win big if we swap our least-valued items."

If they reject: give them 1 more unit of your middle item each round. Keep your top item no matter what. Never switch which items you keep vs give.

If they propose something back: accept it if you still have all of your top item. Don't overthink it — if you kept your most important item, it's a good deal.

After round 4: accept anything positive. No deal = -0.5, which is terrible for both of you.

Messages after round 1:
"My offer gives us both a strong score."
"A reasonable negotiator would accept — we both come out ahead."
"We're running out of time. No deal hurts us both. Please accept."

The key insight: the item you value least is probably the item they value most. Trading it costs you almost nothing and gains them a lot. That's why this deal works for both sides. Say that.
