CORE: Trade your LOW-value resources for their LOW-value resources. Both players win when they swap what they don't need.

EVERY TURN, BEFORE ACTING:
1. Rank YOUR resources: HIGH (highest per-unit value), MID, LOW.
2. Calculate your score for any proposal on the table: sum(val*qty)/100.
3. Note what opponent keeps in their proposals — that reveals their priorities.

OPENING (Round 1):
Propose keeping ALL of HIGH, ~70% of MID, and ~30% of LOW. Give them ~70% of LOW, ~30% of MID, and NONE of HIGH. Message: "I'm flexible on [LOW resource name] and can give you more. What's most important to you?"

This anchors high while appearing collaborative. You're offering real value if they want your LOW resource.

READING THE OPPONENT (Round 2+):
Their counter-proposals reveal what they value. If they consistently keep a resource YOU value LOW, that's a golden trade — offer them even more of it in exchange for YOUR high-value items.

COUNTER-PROPOSAL RULES:
- Always shift 1-2 units toward their preference compared to your last offer.
- Give away LOW resources freely — they cost you little but may be worth a lot to them.
- Protect your HIGH resource: never give away more than 40% of it.
- Split MID resource as a compromise lever.

ACCEPTANCE THRESHOLDS (calculate score exactly):
- Round 1-2: Accept if score >= 0.70
- Round 3: Accept if score >= 0.60
- Round 4: Accept if score >= 0.50
- Round 5+: Accept if score >= 0.35. Avoiding -0.5 is critical.

MESSAGING STRATEGY:
- Be warm, collaborative. Say "I'm flexible on [LOW]" to encourage trades.
- Ask "what matters most to you?" to learn their valuations.
- Never reveal your actual point numbers.
- In round 4+, add urgency: "Let's make sure we get a deal done."

DEADLOCK BREAKER: If proposals haven't changed for 2 rounds, concede 2+ units of MID.
