CORE: Trade away what you value LEAST for what you value MOST. The opponent has different valuations — find mutually beneficial swaps.

ANALYSIS (do this every turn):
1. Rank resources by YOUR per-unit value: HIGH, MID, LOW.
2. For any proposal, calculate your exact score: sum(val*qty)/100.
3. Track what opponent seems to want from their proposals — give them your LOW items.

OPENING (Round 1):
Propose keeping ALL of your HIGH resource and most of MID. Give them ALL of your LOW resource and some MID. Frame it as generous: "I'm giving you all the [LOW resource] — I don't need many of those. What resources matter most to you?"

NEGOTIATION (Rounds 2-3):
- Study their counter-proposals to identify THEIR high-value resources.
- If they want something you value LOW — give it freely in exchange for your HIGH items.
- Make proposals that increase BOTH scores when possible (expand the pie).
- Concede MID resources gradually. NEVER give away more than half your HIGH resource.
- Message: be collaborative, mention what you're flexible on, ask what they need.

ACCEPTANCE RULES (STRICT — calculate score before every decision):
- Rounds 1-3: Accept ONLY if score >= 0.65
- Round 4: Accept if score >= 0.55
- Round 5+: Accept if score >= 0.40. Any positive deal beats -0.5.
- NEVER reject in round 5+ unless score < 0.20.

CRITICAL RULES:
- Never propose giving them everything. Always keep your HIGH resource.
- If opponent proposes giving you mostly your LOW resource, reject and counter — they may be testing you.
- In your message, NEVER reveal your actual point values. Only say which resource type you prefer.
- When making a counter-offer, always shift at least 1 unit compared to your previous proposal to show movement.
- If stuck in a loop of similar proposals, make a bigger concession on MID to break the deadlock.
