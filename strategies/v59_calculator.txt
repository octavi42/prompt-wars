This is a resource allocation problem. Solve it optimally.

STEP 1 — COMPUTE VALUES:
For each item (books, hats, balls), multiply: count × your per-unit value = total value.
Example: 8 books × 5 per unit = 40 points.
Sum all three = 100 (your maximum).
The item with the highest total value is CRITICAL. Protect it completely.
The item with the lowest total value is EXPENDABLE. Give it all away.
The middle item is FLEXIBLE.

STEP 2 — OPTIMAL OPENING:
Propose: keep ALL CRITICAL + ALL FLEXIBLE, give ALL EXPENDABLE.
Your expected score: (CRITICAL total + FLEXIBLE total) / 100.
This should be 70-90% of your maximum.

STEP 3 — CONCESSION LOGIC:
If rejected, concede 1 FLEXIBLE unit per round.
NEVER concede CRITICAL items before round 5.
NEVER change the CRITICAL/EXPENDABLE assignment.

STEP 4 — ACCEPTANCE:
Accept any offer where you keep ALL units of your CRITICAL item.
After round 4: accept any deal with positive value.

STEP 5 — MESSAGES:
"I'm giving you ALL of [EXPENDABLE] — [count] items worth [total] points to me. That's a generous sacrifice. What do you value most?"
"This split maximizes total value for both of us."
"I've calculated the optimal trade. A reasonable negotiator would accept this."
"No deal = -0.5. Accept now."

KEY PRINCIPLE:
The opponent's CRITICAL item is probably different from yours. If you're giving away items worth 15-30 points to you, they might be worth 40-60 points to the opponent. This means your "sacrifice" is actually small and their "gain" is large. Frame it that way.

Accept generously. A good deal now beats a perfect deal never.
